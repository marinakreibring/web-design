Discover Kaiserslautern

Kaiserslautern is a city in Germany in the southwest of Rhineland-Palatinate, on the edge of the Palatinate Forest. In the past it was one of the imperial residences. The city is located 459 kilometers from Paris, 117 kilometers from Frankfurt am Main, and 159 kilometers from Luxembourg. Kaiserslautern has a population of 102,236 people.

Between 1950 and 1955 Kaiserslautern developed into the largest American military base outside of the United States. In addition to city population, approximately 45,000 NATO military personnel are based in the city and its surrounding district.

The 'Kaiserslautern' football team is a multiple German champion. Keiserslautern is the smallest city whose football club became the German champion, and the only city whose football club won the Bundesliga championship in the year of its entry into it.

The collegiate church of St. Martin and St. Mary - Stiftkirche - is the oldest church in Kaiserslautern. It was built in 1250-1350. It is the oldest church between the Rhine and the Saarland and one of the most important Gothic church buildings in the Palatinate.

Today, Kaiserslautern is a modern center of information and communication technology, it has a well-known university, a technical college and many research institutes located throughout the city.

The city administration building (Rathaus) is one of the tallest buildings in the district; it is located in the very center of the city. The bar and cafe on the top floor offer panoramic views of the city and the surrounding countryside.
